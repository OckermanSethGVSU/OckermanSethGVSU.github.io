Research

Over the last 3 years, I have discovered a passion for research. This section lists the projects I've had the privileged of working on. If there is an accompanying repository or resource, it will be linked through the title.

A Case for Dataset Specific Profiling | Summer 2022
Through the Big Ten SROP program, I am a summer ICICLE lab intern under Professor Christopher Stewart at The Ohio State University. I led a project demonstrating the potential dangers of current computer vision benchmarking practices and also trained a GBM to predict final neural network test set accuracy with 82% with only 3% of the training time required to reach convergence. A  paper I wrote detailing my findings can be found on Arxiv. We are currently in the process of writing a full paper for publication at ACM based on my summer findings. 

Early Prediction of Covid Outbreaks using Social Media VARMA Analysis  | 2022 - Present
Funded through the MI STEM Forward grant, I use the trained models I created with my social media mask dataset to survey  Twitter mask trends of specific cities. Using this data, I build a VARMA model that can predict COVID outbreaks with a higher degree of accuracy than existing ARIMA models that use case counts alone.  Coupled with our social media mask dataset, our findings will be submitted to the 2022 IEEE ICMLA conference for consideration of publication. 

Social Media Mask Dataset  | 2020-2022
Overseen by Professor Erin Carrier, I created my own research project. Using an automated pipeline, I created a 116k Twitter image dataset of masked images for the training of convolutional neural networks. I then train different models using other datasets to prove the efficacy of our dataset. I presented my results at Michigan State Universities  Midsure  and Grand Valley State's student scholars day.

Increasing the Accessibility of Video Games  | 2019-2020
My freshmen year, I worked as Professor  Ira Woodring's research assistant. We investigated increasing the accessibility of videos for those with disabilities. My primary duties included compiling helpful resources (academic literature, websites, etc.), summarizing those resources, and contributing towards accessibility  standard demo in C.
